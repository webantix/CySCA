What is CySCA?

CySCA is Australia’s only national cyber security competition. It’s designed to excite, inspire, attract and help Australia’s talented people to become our next generation of cybersecurity professionals.

A partnership of Australian Government, business and academia deliver CySCA each year. In 2015, our partners are the Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet, the Australian Signals Directorate, Cisco Systems Australia and New Zealand, the Commonwealth Bank of Australia, Facebook, HackLabs, Microsoft, PwC and Telstra.

We are also working with over 20 tertiary institutions including the Australian National University, Canberra Institute of Technology, Curtin University, Deakin University, Edith Cowan University, Flinders University, Macquarie University, Murdoch University, Queensland University of Technology, Swinburne University of Technology, University of Melbourne, University of New South Wales, Royal Melbourne Institute of Technology, University of Sydney and the University of Technology Sydney.

Background

As Australians conduct more of their social, consumer and business activities online, there is a corresponding increase in the demand for ICT professionals to manage the digital environment. The Government is committed to working with Australia’s private sector to help boost our cyber security skills base and equip our workforce with the skills necessary to thrive in a digital economy. The Government recognises that developing and sustaining an appropriately skilled ICT workforce will ensure Australia’s future prosperity, security and social wellbeing.

The Cyber Security Challenge Australia:

  • helps build the profile of cyber security and encourage Australians to gain the relevant understanding and skills
  • highlights career opportunities and encourages the development of undergraduates studies in cyber security and ICT
  • exposes competitors to some of Australia’s leading cyber security employers

Rules and eligibility

CySCA is open to full-time Australian university undergraduates and undergraduate-equivalent TAFE students based in Australia. Each team may contain up to four students, and each university/TAFE may register up to four teams.

All teams must be registered by a university/TAFE staff/faculty academic representative.

  • Individual applications will not be considered.
  • Registration does not guarantee entry into the event.

The deadline for universities/TAFE's to register their interest in fielding 1-4 teams is Friday 4 September 2015

Privacy Policy

The Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet only uses personal information collected for the purposes for which it was given to us.

Personal information is collected from you by the Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet (the Department) for the purpose of organising and managing the Cyber Security Challenge and your involvement in it.

The information you provide and images taken of you during the competition may also be used for possible recruitment and publicity purposes, if the Australian Government considers offering you employment.

As part of the above purposes, the Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet may disclose some or all of the information and imagery you provide to:

  • The Department of Defence and its portfolio agencies, the Attorney-General’s Department and its portfolio agencies, the Department of Finance and its portfolio agencies, Telstra, NBN Co. and PwC

When an individual’s email address is received by the Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet because they sent us a message, the email address will only be used or disclosed for the purposes outlined above and it will not be added to a mailing list or used or disclosed for any other purpose without the individual’s consent. Personal information provided to the Department is unlikely to be disclosed to overseas recipients.

Publication

The information you provide may also be used in Cyber Security Challenge promotional material, media coverage and posted online. This use may include, but is not limited to, publishing the personal information and image of the competition winners and runners up. If your personal information or image is published on the internet then it will be accessible to millions of users from all over the world. It can be searched, copied and used by other internet users. If your personal information is published on the internet, the Department has no control over its subsequent use and disclosure.

Security

The Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet takes steps to ensure that the personal information we collect is accurate, up to date and complete. These steps include maintaining and updating personal information when we are advised by individuals that their personal information has changed, and at other times as necessary.

The Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet also takes steps to protect the personal information we hold against loss, unauthorised access, use, modification or disclosure and against other misuse. These steps include limited access for electronic files, securing paper files in locked cabinets and physical access restrictions.

This site does not provide facilities for the secure transmission of information across the Internet. Users should be aware that there are inherent risks in transmitting information across the Internet.

You will not be able to participate in the Cyber Security Challenge:

  • if you do not consent to the Department using or disclosing your information for any of the above purposes;
  • unless the personal information required for registration is given.

The Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet can be contacted on (02) 6271 5111. Alternatively, you may email Cyber Security Challenge team using this address: CyberChallenge@pmc.gov.au

If an individual requests access to the personal information we hold about them, or requests that we change that personal information, we will allow access or make the changes unless we consider that there is a sound reason under the Privacy Act, Freedom of Information Act 1982 (Cth) (FOI Act) or other relevant law to withhold the information. If we do not agree to provide access to personal information the individual may seek a review of our decision or may appeal our decision under the FOI Act. If we do not agree to make requested changes to personal information the individual may make a statement about the requested changes and we will attach this to the record.
